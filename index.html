Abstract

Contactless physiological monitoring is promising, but reliable respiratory-rate estimation from short videos remains difficult when evidence is weak, corrupted by motion, or spectrally ambiguous. CALIBRA treats prediction as an evidence-grounded verification problem rather than a one-shot prompting task. It operates on a structured artifact bundle with lightweight signal-derived candidate rates, and performs routing, specialized vision-language evidence analysis, critic-based consistency checking, targeted repair, and calibrated arbitration with optional abstention. Across multiple backbones, CALIBRA consistently improves over prompting-only baselines while also improving grounding and consistency.

Why CALIBRA

Prompt-only inference can be brittle

Short video segments may contain motion corruption, weak periodicity, or harmonic ambiguity, yet a model can still produce a plausible-looking respiratory rate without strong evidence support.

Verification matters

CALIBRA reframes contactless respiratory-rate estimation as a grounded verification problem: candidate rates are proposed, checked against structured visual evidence, repaired when needed, and only then arbitrated into a final decision.

Reliability matters too

The system can abstain on unreliable segments rather than forcing a weak answer, improving safety and calibration in challenging monitoring conditions.

Method Overview

CALIBRA operates on a structured artifact bundle containing an annotated frame, respiratory signal plot, optical-flow summary, and histogram view, together with two lightweight deterministic candidate respiratory rates. It then performs staged evidence-grounded verification through routing, specialized VLM evidence analysis, critic-based conflict checking, targeted repair, and calibrated arbitration.

Router

Assesses artifact severity, breathing regularity, and disagreement risk to decide whether the segment follows a standard or stricter path.

Specialists

Separate evidence agents analyze waveform, motion, candidate agreement, and visual count evidence from the artifact bundle.

Critic + Repair + Arbiter

The critic checks consistency, the repair stage revisits problematic channels, and the arbiter outputs the final rate, confidence, source, and abstention decision.

Main Results

Across all tested backbones, CALIBRA achieves the lowest MAE, showing that explicit verification is more effective than longer prompting alone. On Gemini-2.5-Flash, CALIBRA reduces MAE from 5.21 to 3.42. On GPT-4o, it reduces MAE from 5.48 to 3.78. Open models also improve consistently, indicating that the framework is not tied to a single proprietary backbone.

Qualitative takeaway

Difficult segments often contain partially informative but mutually inconsistent cues. A prompt-only model may still output a plausible rate, while waveform, histogram, motion summary, and deterministic candidates disagree. CALIBRA reduces these failures by forcing agreement checks before commitment.

Output quality

Beyond numerical error, CALIBRA also improves grounding, consistency, rationale quality, confidence appropriateness, and plausibility in judge-based evaluation.

Efficiency and Ablation

Deployment trade-off

The routed configuration provides the best operating point, retaining most reliability gains without the full overhead of always-on multi-pass execution.

  • Avg. latency: 4.1 s
  • P95 latency: 8.7 s
  • Peak RAM: 1.6 GB
  • Coverage: 93%

Component ablation

The critic–repair loop provides the largest gains. Removing the critic increases MAE from 3.42 to 3.94, while removing repair increases MAE to 3.82. Routing and abstention also provide complementary benefits.

Figure 5. Component ablation of CALIBRA on the best-performing configuration.

Dataset and Setup

We evaluate CALIBRA on an in-house video-based respiratory dataset with 50 RGB recordings captured under diverse breathing patterns, viewpoints, lighting conditions, and motion levels. Recordings are 2.5–3.5 minutes long at 1920 × 1080 and 30 fps, with subject-wise evaluation and synchronized Vernier Go Direct Respiration Belt ground truth.
